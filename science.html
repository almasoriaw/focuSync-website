The Science Behind Focus

How neuroplasticity research and eye tracking inform our focus detection system

Neuroplasticity Framework

Our approach is grounded in cutting-edge neuroplasticity research, particularly drawing from Dr. Andrew Huberman's work on attention and learning readiness. Key concepts include:

  • Neurochemical Gating - Focus creates a neurochemical environment that enables learning. When focused, our brains release epinephrine, acetylcholine, and dopamine that prepare neural circuits for change.
  • Visual Attention and Acetylcholine - Sustained visual attention triggers acetylcholine release, a key neurotransmitter for neural plasticity.
  • EEG Markers of Focus - Gamma (30-50 Hz) and Alpha (8-13 Hz) rhythms serve as indicators of focus and learning readiness.
  • Sustained Focus - Neuroplasticity requires sustained periods of focus (typically 90 minutes), followed by breaks, aligning with ultradian cycles.

Facial Markers of Focus

Based on research by Mark et al., we define focus states along two dimensions:

  • Engagement - Level of participation and attention
  • Challenge - Level of cognitive difficulty experienced

Focus is defined as the state where a person is both highly engaged and challenged, creating optimal conditions for learning and neuroplasticity.

Action Units and Focus States

Our research identified specific facial Action Units (AUs) that correlate with focus states:

Focus State Associated Action Units Correlation
Engagement AU20 (Lip Stretcher), AU25 (Lips Part) Positive
Engagement Gaze Angle, Pitch, AU14 (Dimpler) Negative
Challenge AU5 (Upper Lid Raiser) Positive
Challenge Gaze Angle Direction, Pitch, AU28 (Lip Suck) Negative
Focus AU1 (Inner Brow Raiser), AU2 (Outer Brow Raiser), AU12 (Lip Corner Puller), AU20 (Lip Stretcher) Significant Effect

Eye Movement Features

Based on research by Heba et al., we analyze an extensive set of eye movement features from three types of eye movement events:

  • Fixations - When the eye remains relatively still
  • Saccades - Rapid eye movements between fixation points
  • Post-Saccadic Oscillations - Small movements following saccades

These features provide temporal, positional, and dynamic characteristics of eye movements that correlate with attentional states.

Integration of Research

By combining neuroplasticity theory with specific facial and eye movement markers, our system creates a comprehensive framework for detecting focus states that is grounded in neuroscience and validated through empirical research.
